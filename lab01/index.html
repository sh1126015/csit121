Chow Mein

Created by Anil Shrestha

Ingredients:

  • 200g (7 oz) chow mein noodles (or any other preferred noodles)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 cup sliced cabbage
  • 1/2 cup carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cup bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup onion, sliced
  • 100g (3.5 oz) chicken breast, thinly sliced (optional, can be replaced with shrimp, beef, or tofu for a vegetarian version)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional, can omit for a vegetarian version)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Spring onions and sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. pepare Noodles: Cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Usually, this involves boiling them in water until they are just tender. Drain and set aside. Some recipes recommend rinsing the noodles in cold water to stop the cooking process.
  2. Cook Meat (if using): If you're including chicken, beef, shrimp, or any other meat, heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil in a wok or large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the meat and stir-fry until just cooked through. Remove the meat from the pan and set aside.
  3. Sauté Vegetables: In the same pan, add another tablespoon of oil if needed. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the sliced vegetables (cabbage, carrot, bell pepper, onion) and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they are tender-crisp.
  4. ombine Ingredients: Return the cooked meat to the pan (if using) along with the cooked noodles. Stir everything together.
  5. Season: Add the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), and sesame oil to the pan. Toss well to ensure that the noodles and filling are evenly coated with the sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir-fry for another minute or two.
  6. Serve: Transfer the chow mein to serving plates. Garnish with chopped spring onions and sesame seeds. Serve hot.

Why This is My Favorite Recipe:

Chow mein is favorite food of mine because of it's versatile, flavorful, offers a satisfying mix of textures, is easy to make, serves as comfort food, and can be nutritionally balanced.
